Role Clarity, Capability Preservation, and Reliability

Excerpt for internal discussion (maintenance & reliability)

1) Management

Core responsibilities
  • Set priorities, constraints, and direction
  • Allocate resources and protect reliability work
  • Focus on risk, trends, and long-term asset health
  • Remove systemic barriers
If responsibility ownership shifts
  • Operational decisions move upward
  • Planning stability is reduced
  • Short-term output is prioritized over system health
Long-term system consequences
  • Planning and execution become dependent on management intervention
  • Decision-making capability does not mature at lower levels
  • Reliability performance becomes less predictable over time
References
  • Deming — Out of the Crisis
  • SMRP — Planning & Scheduling Body of Knowledge

2) Leaders / Supervisors

Core responsibilities
  • Ensure work meets standards and expectations
  • Reinforce quality and consistency
  • Identify repeat issues and performance drift
  • Develop capability within the team
If responsibility ownership shifts
  • Planning and scheduling functions are bypassed
  • Job readiness becomes inconsistent
  • Execution depends on individual effort
Long-term system consequences
  • Work quality varies by individual rather than process
  • Leaders spend time compensating instead of improving
  • Variability and emergency work increase
References
  • Reliability Academy — Without Planning & Scheduling You Will Fail
  • Mobley — Maintenance Fundamentals

3) Planners

Core responsibilities
  • Define complete job scope and readiness
  • Ensure materials, tools, and permits are available
  • Estimate labor accurately
  • Build stable, achievable schedules
If responsibility ownership shifts
  • Planning is reduced to date coordination
  • Execution details are over-specified
  • Feedback loops weaken
Long-term system consequences
  • Planning capability becomes administrative rather than analytical
  • Job plans stop improving over time
  • Learning from execution is lost
References
  • Hackman & Oldham — Job Characteristics Theory (overview)
  • Braverman — Labor and Monopoly Capital (overview)

4) Technicians (Skilled Trades)

Core responsibilities
  • Diagnose equipment issues
  • Apply judgment and experience
  • Execute work to standard
  • Identify defects and improvement opportunities
If responsibility ownership shifts
  • Work becomes instruction-driven
  • Diagnostic decisions move away from the field
  • Feedback decreases
Long-term system consequences
  • Diagnostic and problem-solving capability erodes
  • Equipment knowledge becomes concentrated and non-transferable
  • Reliability depends on individuals rather than systems
References
  • Deskilling (overview)
  • Edmondson — The Fearless Organization (overview)

Why Reliability Is Especially Sensitive

Reliability improvement relies on early detection, accurate reporting, consistent standards, and organizational learning. When decision authority migrates away from the point of work, feedback loops weaken and failures can accumulate gradually.
